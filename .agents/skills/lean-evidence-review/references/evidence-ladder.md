# Evidence Ladder

From weak to strong:

1. Polite praise
2. Stated interest
3. Single anecdote
4. Repeated qualitative signal
5. Side-by-side preference
6. Behavioral click / signup / reply
7. Time spent or workflow usage
8. Data sharing or intro commitment
9. Deposit / paid pilot / preorder
10. Structured repeated sample
11. Quantitative result under constraints

## Interpretation Rules

- Praise proves almost nothing.
- Stated interest is weak unless followed by behavior.
- Repeated qualitative signal can justify a better test, not scaling.
- Costly commitment is stronger than survey intent.
- Quantitative data is only strong if the setup, sample, and constraints are clean.

## Bias Checks

Ask:
- Did the user know they were being tested?
- Was the context realistic?
- Was there a real alternative?
- Was the sample only friendly users?
- Was the behavior costly enough to matter?
- Are failures and non-responses recorded?
